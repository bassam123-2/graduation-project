Physical Therapy (Physiotherapy)

Physical therapy is a broad rehabilitation science that aims to restore movement, improve physical function, and enhance overall quality of life. It is ideal for individuals recovering from injuries, surgeries, chronic conditions, or physical limitations due to aging or neurological disorders.

Core Elements of Physical Therapy:

  • Therapeutic Exercise Programs: Personalized exercises designed to improve strength, endurance, flexibility, and coordination.
  • Stretching and Mobility Work: Gentle movements to lengthen muscles and improve joint mobility.
  • Neuromuscular Re-education: Techniques to improve balance, coordination, and body awareness, especially after neurological injuries.
  • Manual Therapy: Often included within physical therapy to assist in soft tissue and joint healing.
  • Physical Modalities: Use of heat therapy, cold therapy, electrical stimulation, ultrasound, or laser therapy to relieve pain and promote healing.
  • Patient Education: Guidance on proper body mechanics, ergonomics, and self-care strategies to prevent future injuries.

Benefits:

  • Speeds up recovery after injuries or surgery.
  • Improves strength, flexibility, and physical performance.
  • Reduces chronic pain and inflammation.
  • Restores movement and mobility in daily activities.
  • Enhances balance and prevents falls in elderly individuals.

Typical Session Duration:

Usually lasts between 30 to 60 minutes per session, depending on the condition and goals.
