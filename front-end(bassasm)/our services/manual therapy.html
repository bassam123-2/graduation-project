Manual Therapy

Manual therapy is a highly specialized, hands-on treatment method performed by skilled therapists who use their hands to assess, diagnose, and treat soft tissues and joint structures. This approach focuses on applying specific techniques to muscles, joints, ligaments, and tendons to improve mobility, reduce pain, and enhance overall physical function.

Common Techniques Include:

  • Joint Mobilization: Gentle, controlled movements to increase joint flexibility and relieve stiffness.
  • Soft Tissue Mobilization (Massage): Techniques that target muscles, fascia, and tendons to break down scar tissue, reduce muscle tightness, and enhance blood circulation.
  • Myofascial Release: A specialized technique aimed at relieving tension in the connective tissue that surrounds muscles.
  • Trigger Point Therapy: Applying direct pressure to painful knots in muscles to relieve tension and reduce referred pain.
  • Stretching: Passive or active stretching techniques to lengthen muscles and improve flexibility.

Benefits:

  • Alleviates chronic and acute pain.
  • Restores joint mobility and range of motion.
  • Promotes faster healing from injuries.
  • Enhances posture and body alignment.
  • Reduces muscle tension and improves circulation.
